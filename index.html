Why executives are moving past chatbots

Delegation, not conversation

The New York Times reports that busy executives are increasingly relying on AI to act like a chief of staff, not a search box. They want systems that can take work off their plate — end to end — without supervision.

Computer use is the inflection point

Google’s introduction of computer use in Gemini 3.5 Flash underscores where the market is headed: agents that can actually click, type, navigate, and complete workflows across real software.

Security and realism matter

Recent reporting has shown that many open‑source agents ship with serious vulnerabilities, especially once they’re allowed to run commands or browse freely. Executives need intentional design, not experiments.
